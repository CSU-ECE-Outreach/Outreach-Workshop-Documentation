Ordering PCBs
================

Once you have designed your PCB, you will need to order it from a manufacturer. There are many PCB manufacturers available, and the process of ordering can vary depending on the manufacturer you choose. Here are some general steps to follow when ordering PCBs:

1. Grab drill and Gerber files from your PCB design software. These files contain all the necessary information for the manufacturer to produce your PCB.

Here's a great guide for KiCAD from JLCPCB: `JLCPCB KiCAD Guide <https://jlcpcb.com/help/article/how-to-generate-gerber-and-drill-files-in-kicad-8>`_

Grabbing files from EasyEDA: `EasyEDA Gerber Guide <https://docs.easyeda.com/en/PCB/Gerber-Generate/>`_

2. Choose a PCB manufacturer. Some popular options include JLCPCB, PCBWay, and OSH Park. Consider factors such as cost, turnaround time, and quality when making your decision.
3. Verify your design files. Most manufacturers will have a design rule check (DRC) tool that you can use to verify that your design meets their manufacturing requirements. This step is crucial to avoid any issues during production. 

I recommend using `Tracespace view <https://tracespace.io>`_

That way you catch any issues you may get before placing your order.

4. Place your order. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for uploading your design files and placing your order. Be sure to double-check all the details, such as quantity, board dimensions, and any special requirements.
